Common Questions

Questions clinicians ask before working with us.

We understand the skepticism. These are the concerns we hear most often — and they're exactly the right questions to ask.

How do you handle client confidentiality in content?+
We never use client stories, case details, or anything that could risk identification. Content is built entirely from your professional expertise — psychoeducation, clinical insights, and your philosophical approach. We include content disclaimers where appropriate.
Will my content sound like me — or like a marketing agency?+
Everything we produce is drawn from conversations with you. We translate, we don't replace. Nothing publishes without your review and explicit sign-off.
Is this appropriate for licensed professionals?+
Athenna was built specifically for licensed clinicians. We understand scope of practice, ethical advertising guidelines, and the professional standards that govern your public communication.
I'm uncomfortable with self-promotion. Is this still for me?+
Most of our clients feel exactly that way. Our approach is education-led visibility — you share your expertise; we structure it so the right people can find it. It feels like teaching, not selling.
How much of my time does this require?+
Minimal. One brainstorm session, one short video call, and a review cycle each month. We handle strategy, writing, design, editing, and scheduling.
Will you make claims I'm not comfortable with?+
Never. We use evidence-informed language, nuanced phrasing, and autonomy-supportive copy: "You can," "Consider," "If it fits." Nothing aggressive or manipulative.
What platforms do you work with?+
Primarily Instagram, LinkedIn, and Facebook. We can discuss other platforms during your discovery call based on where your audience is most active.
Can I pause or adjust if my caseload changes?+
Absolutely. We build flexibility into every engagement. Sustainability means adapting to real clinical life, not forcing a rigid marketing calendar.

The Monthly Workflow

Eight steps. One system. Full clinician control.

A structured, repeatable process designed to minimize your time while maximizing quality and ethical alignment.

1

Brainstorm Blog Topics

Suggest a few potential topics — typically the questions you hear most in practice, common misconceptions, or practical insights your audience could benefit from.

2

Write the Monthly Blog

We produce one 800–1,000 word blog post. Simply share bullet points or an outline, and we'll craft it into a polished post that sounds like you.

3

Extract Social Media Content

From the blog, we identify key ideas that translate into social formats: 6–8 short-form video topics and 6–8 text carousel posts.

4

Review and Approval

You receive the full list of extracted content ideas. Approve concepts, refine language, add context. Your professional voice always comes first.

5

Carousel Content Creation

We design branded carousel posts — clean, professional, visually consistent with your identity.

6

Record Short-Form Video

A brief Zoom recording session. We guide the conversation with targeted questions — keeping content natural, conversational, and authentic.

7

Edit the Video Content

We edit recordings into individual short-form clips. Each focuses on one clear concept, captioned and formatted for social platforms.

8

Schedule and Publish

Content is scheduled to publish gradually throughout the month — consistent presence without requiring ongoing time from you.

Suggested content disclaimers

We help develop appropriate disclaimers for educational content. Here are examples of language we build into your system.

General educational content

"This content is for educational purposes and is not a substitute for professional mental health treatment. If you're in crisis, please contact your local emergency services or crisis line."

Psychoeducational posts

"This information is general in nature and may not apply to your specific situation. Consider consulting with a licensed professional for personalized guidance."

Practice-specific content

"The perspectives shared here reflect [Clinician Name]'s professional approach and are not intended as clinical advice for any individual's specific circumstances."
